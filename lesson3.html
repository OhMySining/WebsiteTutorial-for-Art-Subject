The Land of Promise: Artistic Tapestry of Mindanao

Mindanao, the second-largest and southernmost island in the Philippines, is frequently referred to as the "Land of Promise" due to its vast, untapped natural resources and rich biodiversity.

Beyond its natural wealth, it is a vibrant "Tapestry of Culture," weaving together the artistic traditions.

About this lesson

This lesson invites students to explore the diverse and spiritually rich creative landscape of the Southern Philippines. Mindanao’s art is a brilliant "tapestry" because it weaves together the ancestral traditions of the Lumad (indigenous groups) and the sophisticated aesthetic of Moro (Muslim) culture.

By the end of this lesson, you will be able to:

  • Recognize the unique characteristics, elements, and principles of Mindanaoan arts, such as the Okir design and the T’nalak weave.
  • Explain how Islamic traditions and indigenous Lumad practices shape the functionality and design of Mindanaoan artifacts.
  • Design a Mindanao-inspired craft using local materials and organize a simple virtual or physical exhibit to showcase the work.
