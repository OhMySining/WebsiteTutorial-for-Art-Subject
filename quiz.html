📝 Answer Key

1. B (Bulol) — Guard rice granaries and invoke harvest deities.

2. C (Spanish) — Most visible in church architecture and decorative arts.

3. C (Soul's Journey) — Two figures on a boat symbolize the voyage to the afterlife.

4. B (Moriones) — Bold, fierce masks used in Marinduque religious festivals.

5. B (Marine Life/Local Fibers) — Uses buri palm, bamboo, and shells.

6. C (Through Dreams) — T'boli weavers receive spiritual guidance for patterns.

7. A (Okir/Geometric) — Islamic tradition avoids human figures in favor of complex patterns.

8. B (Harvest Thanksgiving) — Originally a gratitude ritual for agriculture.

9. C (Identity/Movement) — Must represent the region while allowing energetic dance.

10. C (Unified Vision) — Everyone (actors/crew) working together for a single artistic goal.
