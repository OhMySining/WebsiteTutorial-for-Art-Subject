Islands of Creativity: The Arts of MIMAROPA and the Visayas

The arts and crafts of MIMAROPA (Mindoro, Marinduque, Romblon, Palawan) and the Visayas showcase a rich tapestry of Philippine cultural heritage, heavily influenced by local traditions, indigenous materials, and religious beliefs.

These regions are known for functional art, intricate weaving, and distinctive handicrafts.

About this lesson

This lesson explores the vibrant artistic landscape of MIMAROPA (Mindoro, Marinduque, Romblon, and Palawan) and the Visayas region. It highlights how these island provinces serve as a "cultural bridge," where ancient indigenous practices meet the deep historical imprints of Spanish colonial heritage. Art in the central Philippines is a celebration of both the land and the sea, utilizing materials like marble, shells, and palm fibers to tell stories of faith, survival, and community.
